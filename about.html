About the Realm

The game, the maker, and answers to the questions we hear most.

The game

Knight's Realm is a real-time strategy game with charming pixel art and a big heart. You start with a castle, a knight and thirty of everything — and from there it's up to you: send pawns to chop wood, herd sheep and mine gold; raise houses, barracks, archeries, monasteries and towers; and recruit an army of lancers, archers, monks and generals to defend it all.

Standing in your way: goblins. Torch-wavers who set your village alight, barrel goblins who ambush from inside explosive kegs, and TNT goblins lobbing dynamite from their ramshackle towers. Every match takes place on a freshly generated island where four rival castles compete for land, lumber and glory.

The game is built in Godot 4 and is designed to feel at home on both PC and mobile — pick it up for five minutes or lose an evening to "one more island".

The developer

Knight's Realm is an indie game, crafted with love by a very small team — the kind of small where the programmer, the designer and the person who fixes the coffee machine hold a meeting by looking in a mirror. Every unit, building and goblin is placed with care (and immediately set on fire during playtesting).

FAQ

What platforms will Knight's Realm be on?

PC via Steam, plus Android (Google Play) and iOS (App Store). The store pages are in preparation — the buttons on this site will go live as soon as they are.

When does it release?

When it's ready — we're not putting a date on it yet. Follow the news page or our socials for the announcement.

How much will it cost?

Pricing hasn't been finalised. What we can promise: no energy bars, no pay-to-win goblins.

What engine is it built with?

Godot 4 — an excellent open-source game engine we're proud to build on.

Is there multiplayer?

Right now the focus is the single-player experience against goblins and rival AI castles. Anything beyond that will be announced when (and if) it happens.

How can I support the game?

Wishlist it on Steam once the page is live, and follow us on Instagram and YouTube. Sharing the game with a friend who likes castles helps more than you would believe.
